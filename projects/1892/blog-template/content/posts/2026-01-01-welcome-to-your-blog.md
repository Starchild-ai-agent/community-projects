You are looking at a complete, production-ready blog platform. Not a demo, not a starter kit that needs six more decisions before it works. The page you are reading was generated by the same pipeline your posts will use.

Here is the pitch in one sentence: **fork this project, and your Starchild agent becomes your publishing team.** It writes, formats, optimizes, builds, and publishes. You review and approve.

## What You Get Out of the Box

Every feature below is already working in this fork. No plugins, no configuration required.

**Content pipeline**
- Markdown posts with YAML frontmatter, one file per article
- Draft workflow: drafts are rendered for preview but kept out of the homepage, RSS, and search engines until you flip `draft: false`
- Article importer that can pull any web page into the blog as a formatted draft, with CSS scoped so it cannot break the site design
- Automatic reading-time estimates and prev/next navigation

**SEO, fully automated**
- Canonical URLs on every page
- JSON-LD structured data (Article + Organization schema) so search engines understand your content
- XML sitemap and robots.txt, regenerated on every build
- Meta descriptions with sensible fallbacks

**Distribution built in**
- RSS feed, plus a separate feed per tag
- Open Graph cards auto-generated from each post title, so every link you share on X, Slack, or Telegram gets a branded preview image
- A JSON API of your posts (`/api/posts.json`) for anything else you want to build on top

**Analytics without a tracking script**
- Every outbound link is UTM-tagged at build time, with the post slug as `utm_content`
- Your analytics platform can attribute every click to the exact article that drove it
- Zero client-side JavaScript trackers, nothing to slow the page down

**Zero infrastructure**
- The whole site builds to static HTML in under a second
- No database, no server runtime, no framework updates to chase
- The one JavaScript dependency (Chart.js, for posts with charts) is vendored, so nothing loads from a CDN

## How to Fork It

Copy this one line, paste it to your Starchild agent, and you are done:

> **Fork 1892-blog-template, set it up with my brand, and publish our first blog article.**

Your agent forks the code into its workspace, reads the operating manual that ships with it, configures the site, and comes back with a preview link. (You can also fork manually from the Projects tab if you prefer to browse first.)

From then on, you just talk to your agent. Some things you might say:

> "Set up the blog with our company name and brand colors."

Your agent edits `config.py` (site name, tagline, CTA button, brand colors for the social cards) and swaps the logo files in `assets/`.

> "Write a post announcing our new feature, keep it under 800 words."

Your agent writes the markdown, sets the frontmatter, builds the site, and gives you a preview link. You read it, request changes, and approve.

> "Import my article from this URL as a draft."

Your agent runs the importer, which fetches the page, extracts the content, scopes the styles, and files it as a draft for your review.

> "Publish it."

Your agent flips the draft flag, rebuilds, and pushes the site live.

## The Workflow Your Agent Follows

The repo ships with `AGENT.md`, a complete operating manual your agent reads automatically. It covers the build commands, frontmatter reference, draft workflow, import tools, troubleshooting, and a pre-publish validation checklist. You never need to explain the system to your agent; the manual is the system.

The day-to-day loop looks like this:

1. You ask for a post (or the agent drafts one on a schedule you set)
2. The agent writes it as a draft and shares a preview
3. You review in the preview, request edits in plain language
4. On your approval, the agent publishes

Because posts are plain markdown files in git, you also get full version history of everything ever published.

## Customizing the Look

The design system is deliberately small:

- **Colors**: one block of CSS variables at the top of `assets/style.css`
- **Social card colors**: the `OG_*` values in `config.py`
- **Logo and icon**: three files in `assets/` (`icon.png`, `icon.svg`, `wordmark.svg`)
- **Fonts**: the template ships with Google Sans; swap the files in `assets/fonts/` and update two `@font-face` rules

Tell your agent your brand colors and hand it a logo file. It knows where everything goes.

## Under the Hood

For the curious: the entire generator is about 1,000 lines of readable Python with two dependencies (`markdown` and `Pillow`). There is no framework to learn and nothing your agent cannot inspect, explain, or modify. If you want a feature the template does not have, ask your agent to build it.

```
config.py          your site settings (name, URL, colors, CTA)
build.py           the generator
content/posts/     your articles, one markdown file each
templates/         four small HTML templates
assets/            styles, fonts, logos
public/            the built site (regenerated every build)
```
